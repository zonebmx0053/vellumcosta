The profile of the Nordic tourist on the Costa del Sol

January 2026 | Vellum Analysis Team | 4 min read

This market seeks quality and comfort. What they expect to find in a property and how to adapt your home to their expectations. Historically, the British market has been the undisputed engine of the Costa del Sol. However, recent data shows an unstoppable rise of a very interesting and stable profile: the Scandinavian tourist and investor (Sweden, Norway, Finland, and Denmark).

Quality tourism and long stays

The Nordic client visiting the Costa del Sol is not looking for mass tourism. They are families, professionals, and entrepreneurs fleeing the harsh winters of their countries. This factor is crucial, as they are the key to reducing the seasonality of vacation homes.

While the traditional tourist rents for one or two weeks in August, the Nordic client seeks stays of 4 to 8 weeks between November and March, allowing owners to generate solid income in historically lower-demand months.

What does the Nordic client look for in a property?

To attract this segment, the property must not only be well-located but also adapted to their tastes. At Vellum Costa, we audit homes looking to meet these key requirements:

  • Design and lighting: They reject cluttered or outdated decoration. They look for clean spaces, neutral colors (whites, light woods), and maximizing natural light.
  • Flawless connectivity: Many are professionals or remote workers. High-speed fiber optic Wi-Fi is non-negotiable.
  • Efficient climate control: They are used to perfectly insulated homes. Having efficient climate control systems or underfloor heating for the winter months is a highly valued factor.
"Adapting a property to Scandinavian tastes can significantly improve occupancy and income during the winter months."
